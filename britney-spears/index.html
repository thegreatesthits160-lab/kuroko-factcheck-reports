ブリトニー・ジーン・スピアーズは1981年12月2日、アメリカ南部・ミシシッピ州マコームで生まれた。父ジェイミー（ジェームズ・パーネル・スピアーズ）と母リンの間の3人きょうだいの真ん中で、兄ブライアンと妹ジェイミー・リンがいる。生まれてまもなく一家は隣のルイジアナ州ケントウッドへ移り、彼女はこの南部の小さな町で育った。1984年頃には地元のダンス教室に通い始め、発表会でひとりだけのソロを任されるなど、幼い頃から人前で歌い踊る素質を見せていた。

Britney Jean Spears was born on December 2, 1981, in McComb, Mississippi, the middle of three children of Jamie (James Parnell Spears) and Lynne, between older brother Bryan and younger sister Jamie Lynn. Soon after her birth the family moved to the small southern town of Kentwood, Louisiana, where she grew up. Around 1984 she began dance lessons and was singled out for solo numbers at recitals, showing an early gift for performing.

At age eight she auditioned in Atlanta for the Disney Channel's revival of "The Mickey Mouse Club." Casting director Matt Casella rejected her as too young — but recognized her talent and introduced her to New York talent agent Nancy Carson. On Carson's advice, Lynne and her daughters moved to a sublet apartment in New York, where Britney attended the Professional Performing Arts School, landed her first professional job as the understudy for the lead role of Tina Denmark in the off-Broadway musical "Ruthless!," appeared in TV commercials, and competed on "Star Search" in 1992 (see the reference video: at ten years old she sings "Love Can Build a Bridge").

In December 1992 she auditioned again for "The Mickey Mouse Club" and made the cast. Her castmates included Justin Timberlake — later her boyfriend — Christina Aguilera, and future actors Ryan Gosling and Keri Russell. When the show was cancelled in 1994 she returned to Kentwood and went back to ordinary school life at Parklane Academy in nearby McComb.

The turning point came in 1997. While a plan was underway for her to join Innosense, a girl group backed by Lou Pearlman of Backstreet Boys fame, her mother asked family friend and entertainment lawyer Larry Rudolph for advice, sending him a tape of Britney singing over a Whitney Houston karaoke track along with photos. Rudolph chose to pitch her to labels as a solo act and had her record a proper demo of an unused song. She flew from Kentwood to New York for a same-day round of four labels; three passed, arguing that audiences wanted groups like the Backstreet Boys and the Spice Girls, not "another Madonna." At the fourth, Jive Records, she sang Whitney Houston a cappella in front of the assembled A&R staff — and two weeks later Jive called back with a deal (June 1997). Her debut single would be recorded at Cheiron Studios in Stockholm with songwriter-producer Max Martin. Her worldwide debut was just over a year away.

Reading the former as the casting date and the latter as the broadcast debut reconciles the two, but no definitive primary source has been confirmed. Note also that some Japanese-language articles say she was "born in Kentwood"; correctly, she was born in McComb and raised in Kentwood.
